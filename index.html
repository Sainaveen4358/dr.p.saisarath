Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ's):

vascular surgeon is a medical specialist who diagnoses, treats, and manages conditions related to the vascular system, which includes the arteries and veins that circulate blood throughout the body (excluding the heart and brain). Vascular surgeons treat a wide range of vascular diseases and conditions, from varicose veins to life-threatening aneurysms and arterial blockages.

An endovascular surgeon is a specialist who performs minimally invasive procedures within the blood vessels (hence "endo-" meaning "inside" and "vascular" referring to blood vessels) to treat a variety of vascular conditions. Endovascular surgery is a subspecialty within vascular surgery and involves techniques that allow surgeons to work inside arteries and veins using catheters, stents, and other specialized tools, typically guided by imaging technology like fluoroscopy (live X-ray).

Vascular doctors, or surgeons, focus on treating vascular issues through diagnosis, treatment, and surgery to improve mobility and quality of life.

Vascular surgeons perform various procedures based on the condition and the severity of the vascular disease. Common procedures include: Arterial Disease Treatment,Venous Disease Treatment,Lymphatic Disease Treatment,Diagnostic Services.

Vascular surgeries are generally safe, especially with advancements in minimally invasive techniques, which have reduced risks and recovery times. However, like any surgical procedure, vascular surgeries carry some risks, such as bleeding, infection, and blood clots. Minimally invasive procedures like angioplasty, stenting, and endovenous ablation typically allow for quick recovery, often requiring only a few days of rest. Open surgeries, like bypass surgery, may require a longer hospital stay and recovery period. Your vascular surgeon will discuss the risks, benefits, and expected recovery time for your specific procedure.

Most vascular procedures are designed to minimize discomfort, and many are done using local anesthesia or sedation, so you’ll feel little to no pain during the procedure itself. For minimally invasive procedures like angioplasty or endovenous ablation, patients often experience only mild discomfort and are able to go home the same day with minimal pain. Open surgeries may involve more recovery time, but pain management is a priority.
